Visualization of the Deformation of 3D Objects for Use by Virtual Prosthetic Limbs

I worked in the Brain Computer Interface laboratory of Dr. Andrew Schwartz (University of Pittsburgh) developing an algorithm to be used in projects evaluating volitional strength required for 3D movement of a robotic arm under different and random levels of resistance. These robotic arms are being used in experimental primates and humans with recording and stimulating microarray electrodes implanted in their brains.

Using MatLab and HOOPS, I created a virtual space in which a virtual hand can realistically deform an object by exerting a force on it. The program registers the collection of forces applied to the object and using Finite Element Analysis displays the deformed object at each time step and gives inertial feedback to the hand. The code is intended to be used to help people in need of prosthetic limbs to understand and calibrate the feeling of the force feedback in a virtual environment using a Brain Computer Interface before they use a real, delicate expensive, robotic limb.

Sentence Generator Webapp

I created a grammatically correct English language sentence generator that randomly creates sentences using parts of speech analysis. The program uses predefined sentence structures and combines them in different ways with conjunctions and subordinate clauses.

As a part of Carnegie Mellon's 15-112: Fundamentals of Programming and Computer Science class, I used this sentence generator to create a website that students can use to learn grammar.

CUDA-Accelerated Lattice Boltzmann Simulator

I used the CUDA and OpenGL packages to graphically accelerate the Lattice-Boltzmann Method (LBM) Algorithms. With parallelization and efficient memory usage, considerable optimization and efficiency is possible with LBM simulation. In this simulation, constant 2-dimensional fluid flow through a medium was modeled, and at every lattice node the fluid’s curl, probabilistic velocity vectors, and pressure are calculated and available to be plotted on the screen. Work performed in collaboration with Tom Scherlis

Modeling Diffusion Rates of a Gas in an Enclosed Space

Identified a relationship between the pressure of a certain gas and diffusive flux in a closed system. With Fick’s First Law of Diffusion as basis for calculations, we used a first order differential equation to discern ideal, theorized relationship between the two parameters. We designed and performed an experiment utilizing diffusion of CO2 into air, confirming the theoretical predictions. Worked in collaboration with two high school seniors: Chirag Kulkarni and Haoran Fei.
